# Aru Teacher Guide

This guide helps a facilitator teach Aru without turning the first lesson into grammar overload.

The teaching target is practical confidence: learners should leave with sentences they can read, edit, and extend.

## Teaching Principles

- Start with `ra`, not with terminology.
- Add `ke` only after learners can read a predicate.
- Teach relations through movement and location examples.
- Teach `le` as a frame before teaching relative phrases.
- Keep every learner example short enough to repair together.

## First 20 Minutes

```text
na ra waro.
na ra sela ke luma.
malo ra maru.
ta ra kani ke pani?
```

Ask learners to identify:

- who or what the sentence is about;
- where `ra` splits the sentence;
- whether `ke` marks an object;
- which root carries the main assertion.

## Common Corrections

### Missing `ra`

Wrong:

```text
na waro.
```

Repair:

```text
na ra waro.
```

### Missing `ke`

Wrong:

```text
na ra sela luma.
```

Repair:

```text
na ra sela ke luma.
```

### Too Much Nesting

Repair long attempts by splitting them into two short sentences.
