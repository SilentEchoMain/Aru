# Examples

This file contains beginner lessons, validated examples, dialogues, short texts, and smoke phrase translations for **Aru v1.0.0**.

The full 150-entry phrasebook is in [PHRASEBOOK.tsv](PHRASEBOOK.tsv).

## Lessons

### Lesson 1: `ra` and `ke`

`ra` marks the boundary between what we talk about and what we say about it.

```text
na ra waro.
I speak.
```

```text
malo ra maru.
The house is big.
```

`ke` marks a direct object.

```text
na ra sela ke luma.
I see light / the sun.
```

```text
mene ra maku ke malo.
The person builds a house.
```

Practice:

```text
ta ra lora ke na.
You hear me.
```

```text
na ra raku ke sipu.
I write a text.
```

### Lesson 2: Time, Negation, and Commands

Time goes after `ra`.

```text
pa -> past
nu -> now / present
po -> future
```

```text
na ra pa tanu.
I came.
```

```text
na ra nu kani ke pani.
I am eating food.
```

```text
na ra po naka.
I will go.
```

`no` stands before what it negates.

```text
na ra no kani ke pani.
I do not eat food.
```

With tense, default event negation is:

```text
na ra pa no tanu.
I did not come.
```

Commands use `o`.

```text
o naka.
Go.
```

```text
o no naka.
Do not go.
```

```text
ta o lora ke na.
You, listen to me.
```

### Lesson 3: Noun Groups and Relations

In Aru, the main word comes first. Modifiers follow.

```text
mene nela
good person
```

```text
malo maru
big house
```

```text
pani welo
hot food
```

`ne` is a weak relation.

```text
malo ne na
my house
```

```text
naru ne waro
rule of speech / grammar
```

Use stronger relation words when the relation matters.

```text
to -> place / contact
ko -> direction / goal
so -> source
we -> accompaniment / means
```

```text
na ra to malo.
I am at the house.
```

```text
na ra naka ko malo.
I go to the house.
```

```text
na ra naka so malo.
I go from the house.
```

```text
na ra salo we tulu.
I work with a tool.
```

### Lesson 4: Context and Content

`le` marks a context frame.

```text
X le, Y
in the context of X, Y is true
```

Time:

```text
temu luma nu le, na ra waro we ta.
Today I speak with you.
```

Condition:

```text
etu ta ra naka le, na ra naka.
If you go, I go.
```

Topic:

```text
malo ne na le, ta ra naka ko i.
As for my house, you go to it.
```

Content uses `ke i le`.

```text
na ra sona ke i le, u ra pa tanu.
I know that they came.
```

```text
ta ra rima ke i le, na ra naka.
You want me to go.
```

Cause clauses use `so etu i le`.

```text
na ra no naka so etu i le, u ra nilo.
I do not go because it is cold.
```

### Lesson 5: Questions and Relative Phrases

`ka` marks a yes/no question.

```text
ka ta ra kani?
Are you eating?
```

`se` marks an unknown element.

```text
se ra naka?
Who is going?
```

```text
ta ra sela ke se?
What do you see?
```

```text
ta ra to se?
Where are you?
```

Relative phrases use `re` and `ri`.

```text
mene re ri ra maku ke malo
person who builds a house
```

```text
malo re na ra sela ke ri
house that I see
```

```text
mene re na ra lano ke pani ko ri
person to whom I give food
```

Use short relative phrases when possible.

### Lesson 6: Style and Short Texts

Aru prefers short clear sentences.

Less preferred:

```text
mene re ri ra sona ke i le, u ra pa tanu, ra nela.
```

Preferred:

```text
mene ra sona ke i le, u ra pa tanu.
mene i ra nela.
```

Short self-introduction:

```text
lani ne na ra ya Mira.
na ra mene.
na ra nema ke ya Aru.
na ra rima waro lisu.
```

Translation:

```text
My name is Mira.
I am a person.
I study Aru.
I want to speak simply.
```

Main rule:

```text
Use the smallest structure that keeps the meaning clear.
```

## Mini Corpus

```text

### Understanding and Ability

```

```text

```

### Movement and Giving

```text

```

```text

```

```text

I go because of cold.
```

```text

I go from the house.
```

```text

```

```text

```

### Relative Phrases

```text
mene re ri ra maku ke malo, ra nela.
The person who builds a house is good.
```

```text
malo re na ra sela ke ri, ra maru.
The house that I see is big.
```

### Comparison

```text
malo i ra maru pasu malo ena.
This house is bigger than that house.
```

```text
malo i ra maru sama pasu malo ena.
This house is as big as that one.
```

```text
malo i ra maru pasu ora.
This house is the biggest.
```

## Dialogues

### Meeting

```text
na ra waro we ta.
ta ra lora ke na?
```

I speak with you.
Do you hear me?

```text
na ra lora ke ta.
lani ne ta ra se?
```

I hear you.
What is your name?

```text
lani ne na ra ya Mira.
lani ne ta ra se?
```

My name is Mira.
What is your name?

```text
lani ne na ra ya Aru.
na ra rima nema ke waro ne ta.
```

My name is Aru.
I want to learn your speech.

### At The House

```text
malo ne na ra kima.
ta ra rima naka ko i?
```

My house is near.
Do you want to go to it?

```text
ka malo ra welo?
```

Is the house hot?

```text
no. malo ra nilo mini.
tune wai ra welo.
```

No. The house is slightly cold.
But the water is hot.

### Cause

```text
ta ra pa no tanu so etu se?
```

Why did you not come?

```text
na ra pa no tanu so etu i le, roka ra tanu.
```

I did not come because rain came.

```text
temu luma po le, ta o tanu.
```

Come tomorrow.

```text
na ra po tanu.
```

I will come.

## Texts

### About Aru

```text
lani ne waro i ra ya Aru.
ya Aru ra waro lisu.
waro i ra no ama ke naru maru.
waro i ra ama ke naru mini.
mene ra pawa waro nela so etu naru lisu.
```

This speech is named Aru.
Aru is simple speech.
This speech does not have big rules.
This speech has small rules.
A person can speak well because of simple rules.

### A Short Day

```text
temu luma nu le, na ra waki.
na ra kani ke pani mini.
na ra taka ke tulu.
na ra naka ko malo ne melo.
melo ne na ra lano ke wai ko na.
na ra waro we melo.
temu luma pini le, na ra tanu ko malo ne na.
na ra miso.
```

Today I wake / begin the day.
I eat a little food.
I take a tool.
I go to my friend's house.
My friend gives me water.
I speak with my friend.
At the end of the day, I come to my house.
I sleep.

### Clear Speech

```text
waro lisu ra nela.
waro maru ra pawa, tune waro maru ra pawa keto.
etu mene ra no sona ke waro le, waro i ra no nela.
o waro mini.
o maku ke naru mini.
o lora ke mene.
```

Simple speech is good.
Big speech can be powerful, but big speech can be harmful.
If a person does not understand speech, that speech is not good.
Speak briefly.
Make small rules.
Listen to people.

## Smoke Phrases

| Prompt | Aru | Note |
|---|---|---|
| I lost the tool. | `na ra pa no ama ke tulu.` | Aru uses "no longer have" for basic losing. |
| If it is cold tomorrow, I will stay in the house. | `etu temu luma po ra nilo le, na ra po mota to malo.` | Conditional frame with `etu ... le`. |
| Who gave you this name? | `se ra pa lano ke lani i ko ta?` | `se` fills the unknown subject. |
| I want to know why they left. | `na ra rima sona ke i le, u ra pa weki so etu se.` | Cause question embedded as content. |
| The person who sees the house gives me water. | `mene re ri ra sela ke malo, ra lano ke wai ko na.` | Relative phrase with subject `ri`. |
| This tool is better than that tool. | `tulu i ra nela pasu tulu ena.` | Regular comparison. |
| Do not go into the cold house. | `o no naka ko insa malo nilo.` | Direction into an interior. |
| We speak simply, but we do not know all rules. | `na mun ra waro lisu, tune na mun ra no sona ke naru ora.` | Sentence-level `tune`. |
| Someone came from a far place. | `mene se ra pa tanu so tera para.` | Indefinite `se`. |
| I see that you are building a new house. | `na ra sela ke i le, ta ra maku ke malo sini.` | Content phrase with `ke i le`. |

## Stress Test Results

- `ne` boundaries are defined by strong boundaries: `ra`, `ke`, `le`, `re`, `an`, `alo`, `sia`, `tune`, `pasu`, pause, end.
- Long `re` phrases are grammatical but discouraged by the style guide.
- `ke i le` remains the standard content clause construction.
- `an` and `alo` connect the nearest same-type groups.
- `to`, `ko`, `so`, `we`, and weak `ne` may act as predicates after `ra`.
- `malo ne na`, `malo ko na`, and `malo to na` are distinct: related to me / for me / at me or by me.
